AI,
Sensemaking,
& Decision‑Making
in Crisis Contexts

Crisis situations increasingly involve the circulation of complex information — real-time data streams, scientific modelling, risk assessments, and community-based knowledge. Around the world, communities and institutions face a growing range of crises, from climate-related events and public health emergencies to geopolitical instability.

This one-day workshop examines how AI participates in crisis-related sensemaking and decision-making, and considers the implications for designing interactive systems in high-stakes contexts. It invites participants to articulate design challenges, share emerging approaches, and identify future research directions.

§ 01

Workshop Themes

01

AI in Crisis Sensemaking

  • How does AI enter existing practices of crisis sensemaking?
  • How do AI-generated outputs shape interpretation and situational understanding?
  • What roles do AI systems play alongside human judgement?
  • How do different actors interpret AI-supported information?
02

Designing for Uncertainty and Decision-Making

  • How can interactive systems communicate uncertainty in crisis contexts?
  • What design approaches support interpretation rather than only prediction?
  • How might design influence how risks and possibilities are understood?
  • What challenges arise when AI informs high-stakes decisions?
03

Participation & Responsibility in AI-Supported Decisions

  • How does AI affect participation in crisis-related decision-making?
  • How are responsibility and accountability negotiated when AI is involved?
  • What design considerations arise when multiple actors rely on AI insights?
  • How can design support inclusive and transparent decision processes?

§ 03

Call for Participation

We invite researchers and practitioners interested in AI, crisis contexts, and design to submit position papers or pictorials. Participants may come from areas including crisis informatics, civic technologies, speculative design, and AI-supported decision systems.

We welcome both accounts of implemented systems and speculative design proposals. Work that is exploratory, in-progress, or critically engaged with the themes is especially encouraged. Submissions should follow the ACM DIS 2026 format and will be reviewed by the organising committee.

Accepted participants will give a brief presentation at the workshop, and will be invited to contribute to a joint post-workshop publication or collaborative follow-up.

POS
Position Papers

2–4 pages. Articulate a design challenge, share an approach, or frame a research question related to the themes.

PIC
Pictorials

2–4 pages. Visual explorations, design probes, or speculative scenarios engaging with AI in crisis contexts.

§ 05

Anticipated Outcomes

01 /

Discussing and comparing design approaches for AI-supported sensemaking and decision-making in crisis contexts, including both implemented systems and speculative design proposals.

02 /

Examining current and emerging applications of AI tools in crisis planning — forecasting, information synthesis, and decision support — and considering their design implications.

03 /

Exploring future design approaches for AI-supported sensemaking, particularly how systems might better support interpretation, deliberation, and communication under uncertainty.

04 /

Clarifying the scope and concerns of this emerging area of design research within HCI and interaction design.

05 /

Creating a basis for future publications and collaborations, including joint papers, panels, or follow-up workshops.
